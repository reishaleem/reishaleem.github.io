About Me

I am a 22 year old software developer studying Computer Science at Ohio State University. The reason why I decided to become a software developer is simple: it's fun. I love coding and am passionate about software and technology in general. My biggest interests are in full-stack web development. The front-end allows me to be creative with UI, be clever with UX, and think hard about problems that would come with any project. The back-end comes with critical thought of designing the database, models, and writing efficient queries. And that's only the tip of the iceberg! There are countless other pieces to web development and software development in general. And that's the best part about coding - the sky is the limit in terms of what you can do. It truly is for everybody.

Another passion of mine is teaching. As an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant, I get the opportunity to work with students just starting to code, as well as students who are much better than I am. It gives me the opportunity to help others learn, while also learnings from others. And most importantly, it connects me with people, creating new relationships that I never would have had. And what's better than coding with friends?

Outside of coding, I love watching basketball and soccer, watching TV, reading books, and hanging out with my friends.

Projects

Projects

Here are a few projects that I've worked on. Try clicking one to view the project or code! Or, head to my GitHub!

The Exceptional Outliner

A React, Spring Boot, and MongoDB application that allows users to sign up and fill worlds with wiki-styled articles using a rich text editor. The app also features an integrated Todo list.

The Generator

A Python random name generator that uses a list of names in an alphabet to calculate a probability matrix for bigrams to generate 'better' names. The user can provide feedback for each name generated to continue improvement. I hope to integrate this into the Exceptional Outliner soon.

OSU Book Exchange

A Rails app that allows users to sign up and create listings for textbooks. They can also search for textbooks based on a variety of factors. This app was created for class in a group of 4 students.

Todo List

A React app that allows users to create tasks and, based on due date, categorizes them as priority, short term, and long term. Users can also mark tasks as completed or pin tasks that they would like to see on the front page. This is the Todo List that is integrated into the Exceptional Outliner.

Advanced Energy Vehicle (AEV)

A class project involving building an AEV from 3D printed parts and coding an Arduino engine to take the AEV through a course, stop at a gate, collect cargo, then reverse back through, before stopping smoothly at the finish.

Picture Rater

A Flask app that was made for Hack OHI/O that accepts images and judges which picture would gain the most likes on social media. The app uses Azure API, Reddit API, and OpenCV. Our team placed 3rd in the Microsoft Challenge out of 15 teams.
