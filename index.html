WELCOME

My name is Reis. I am 20 years old, and I am a Junior studying Computer Science at The Ohio State University. I love coding, and I am always trying to learn more about programming languages, concepts, and good practices. One of my favorite things about coding is how much fun the learning process can be. My strongest language is Java, but my biggest interest is getting into Web Development.

Inside the classroom, the most fundamental classes I have taken are Discrete Structures, Data Structures and Algorithms and the Software Sequence. Learning algorithms was a lot of fun, and the introduction of time complexity has vastly improved my coding ability. The Software Sequence teaches fundamental Computer Science Principles through Java. I am now a Teaching Assistant for this sequence. This heavily involves helping students learn good coding practices, as well as breaking down conceptual ideas from class in different ways to suit their learning style and understanding of the concept. Helping with these classes showed me how much fun it can be to help others, and it has become a strong passion of mine. My favorite part of the job is the great relationships and connections I have made. Be sure to learn more about these courses through the links above. Through other classes I have taken, I have basic knowledge of The C Programming Language and AT&T syntax x-86 assembly. A complete list of relevant coursework as well as other important information can be found on my resumé.

Outside of the classroom, I enjoy hanging out with friends, watching sports, and playing video games. I played soccer growing up, and it has by far become my favorite sport to watch. Basketball is another sport I love to watch. Along with watching sports, I am involved in intramural sports at Ohio State. Even outside the classroom, coding is something I enjoy, and coding with friends is another way to make the learning process even more fun!
